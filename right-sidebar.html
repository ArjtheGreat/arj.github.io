Mask Advice

Face masks are critical to stopping the coronavirus as they block germs containing the virus from entering the respiratory system (nose and mouth especially). However, they only work when applied onto the face properly, protecting all the vulnerable areas. 

Making Homemade Masks by the CDC

  1. Use a bandanna or other square of cloth measuring about 20 inches on each side.
  2. Fold it in half. Then fold the top third down and the bottom third up so you have a long rectangle.
  3. Slide the rectangle through two rubber bands or elastic hair ties until they're about 6 inches apart.
  4. Fold the sides of the fabric in toward the middle, and tuck the ends together.
  5. Hook the elastics over your ears and adjust the fabric so it's snug against your face but not too tight.

How to Wear a Mask

Wearing your mask snugly will ensure that your mask is covering the majority of your face exposing no weak spots.

  1. If you have a storebought mask or a homemade mask, make sure that you have ties or ear loops to prevent the mask from falling off.
  2. If the mask fits well, then the top should be located just below your eyes, the bottom curving around to the bottom of your chin.
  3. If not, MAKE SURE THAT YOUR NOSE AND MOUTH ARE AT LEAST COVERED!  It is too big of a risk to take, and you are better off trying a new size or not going outside at all.

How to Remove a Mask

Remember that things you touch (like groceries, doorknobs) can harbor the coronavirus, sometimes for days.

  
  1. The first thing you should do before taking off your mask is to remove and trash your gloves, then wash your hands.
  2. Afterward, remove the left ear loop and slowly peel the mask from your face, subsequently detaching the right loop.
  3. If the mask is disposable, throw it away immediately and wash your hands again.
  4. If you follow these steps, then you will greatly decrease the chance of getting coronavirus.
